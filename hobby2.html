Cooking

Origin

I learned to cook at an early age. It started my mom giving me small tasks to help with cooking such as prepping things for meals. I learned to cook on the stove first and then I began to learn to make dishes in the oven. Cooking was a consistent activity that I participated in throughout the years.

My First Dish

My first cooking meal started with breakfast. I learned how to scramble eggs, cook bacon, and make grits.

Take Your Time

One thing I learned early about cooking is that it is important to take your time. It may seem convenient to cook your dishes on high heat, but it actually could create a negative effect on your dish. It can affect the impact of your flavor as well as the texture of your dishes.

Cooking Failures

Lack of Focus = Burned Results

When cooking, it is important to be focused on your dish. There are down times in cooking, but you must be aware of the time. Once I started a dish and I forgot to lower my stove temp. In the process I dozed off and overslept, which resulted to a burnt dish.

Baking

When I began to learn to bake, I mainly baked savory dishes such as casseroles and pasta dishes. I haven't really created any desserts in my baking journey, except for a sweet potato pie. It was pretty decent for my first attempt.

Motivation When Cooking

When cooking my motivation for taste, is if it tastes like how my family makes their dishes, then I look for ways to make the dish my own.
